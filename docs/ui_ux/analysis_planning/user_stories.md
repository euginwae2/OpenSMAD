Based on the provided resource documents, here are the key user stories for the OpenSMAD platform.

### **1. User Roles**

- **Agile Innovator (e.g., Startup CTO/Engineer):** Needs low-cost, fast iteration, credible outputs for investors, and a resilient process not dependent on individual experts. Primary motivators: Speed to market and capital efficiency.
- **Educator & Bridge-Builder (e.g., Professor/Researcher):** Needs accessible, teachable tools for students and extensibility for research. Primary motivators: Student success, research capability, and industry relevance.
- **Enterprise Systems Guardian (e.g., Senior Manager/Engineer):** Needs traceability, compliance, integration with corporate digital engineering tools, and risk reduction. Primary motivators: Auditability, risk mitigation, and process efficiency.

### **3. Opportunities for Enhancement (Optional)**

- **For US-02:** Future enhancement could allow trade studies to incorporate **third-party high-fidelity plugins from the Marketplace** (e.g., a proprietary thermal analysis plugin) as part of the batch loop.
- **For US-07:** Future enhancement could include an **"Assumptions Log" pane** within the dashboard, showing the key assumptions driving the current numbers (e.g., "Cost based on NASA QuickCost model, 2025 dollars").
- **For US-04:** Future enhancement for the Enterprise tier could include **watermarking and viewership analytics** on shared links to track contractor engagement.
- **For US-05:** Enhancement could involve **interactive, gamified tutorials** within the example mission, guiding students through specific "what-if" scenarios.
